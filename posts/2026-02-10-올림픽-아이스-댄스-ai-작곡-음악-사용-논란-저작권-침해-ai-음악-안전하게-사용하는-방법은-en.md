# AI Music Copyright Controversy at the Olympics: How to Safely Use AI-Generated Music for Ice Dancing and Avoid Infringement

- A Czech ice dancing duo, Katerina Mrazkova and Daniel Mrazek, faced unforeseen issues at the Olympics after using music composed by AI.
- This incident highlights the potential copyright infringement issues stemming from LLM (Large Language Model) based AI music generators.
- It demonstrates that AI can not only 'generate' music but also potentially plagiarize existing songs.
- Caution is necessary when using AI music. Consider the following:
    - **Copyright Verification**: Always verify copyright issues before using AI-generated music. Thoroughly review it for similarities to existing songs.
    - **AI Service Terms of Service**: Carefully read the terms of service of the AI music generation service, paying close attention to copyright clauses.
    - **Original Data Source Verification**: Check the source of the data that the AI learned from to minimize the possibility of copyright infringement.
    - **Enhance Creativity**: Instead of using AI-generated music as is, it is better to add creativity through additional editing or arrangement.
