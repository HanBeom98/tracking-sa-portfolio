## 🔍 Deep Dive

Therefore, it is crucial to enhance the competitiveness of the Korean data center industry while simultaneously exploring avenues for global collaboration. The situation requires a balanced approach to ensure Korean companies can thrive both domestically and internationally. The slowdown in US data center development might also lead to increased demand and higher prices for existing data center capacity in other regions, indirectly affecting the global pricing landscape for cloud services. Furthermore, Korean cloud providers might see an opportunity to market themselves as secure and sovereign alternatives to US-based giants, potentially gaining market share in specific sectors.
