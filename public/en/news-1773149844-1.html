AI in Healthcare: A Liability Dump on Doctors?

The Medscape article sends shivers down my spine. Doctors are at risk of becoming 'liability sinks' for errors made by LLM-based AI healthcare systems. This isn't just a warning; it's a harbinger of disaster disguised as medical AI.

The healthcare industry is currently a feeding frenzy for AI adoption, driven by the scent of money. Increased efficiency, cost reduction? Sure, those are sweet promises. But lurking beneath is a sinister motive: 'liability evasion.' Who will be held responsible when AI makes a misdiagnosis? The developers who designed the AI? The data scientists who trained the algorithms? Absolutely not. Ultimately, all responsibility will be shifted to the doctors wielding the scalpel.

The problem is that AI systems themselves are 'black boxes.' Doctors have no way of knowing why a particular diagnosis was made or on what basis the decision was reached. Just as ChatGPT can spout nonsense, AI can produce erroneous results. But because the reasons cannot be explained, doctors will be left defenseless, suffering in courtrooms with patients.

This situation has occurred before. When automated financial trading systems caused errors, resulting in massive losses for investors, the engineers who designed the systems conveniently disappeared, and the financial institutions ultimately bore the responsibility. Medical AI is highly likely to follow the same path.

What's even more concerning is that the 'design' of medical AI is solely focused on making money. Patient safety and ethical considerations are secondary. Data bias, algorithm opacity, and security vulnerabilities are just some of the numerous problems that need to be addressed, yet regulatory authorities are sitting idly by.

Furthermore, the medical AI market has already become prey for giant tech companies. Corporations like Google, IBM, and Microsoft, armed with vast capital and data, are aggressively seeking to dominate the healthcare market. They have no intention of taking responsibility for the problems their AI systems may cause. Their attitude is simply to make money.

If this continues, healthcare settings will become graveyards for doctors covering up AI errors. Patients will become experimental subjects for unproven AI technologies, and the entire healthcare system will collapse. It's not too late to implement strong regulations on the adoption of AI medical technology, clarify accountability, and, above all, establish ethical guidelines that prioritize 'patient safety.' Otherwise, we will witness a medical disaster created by AI. Whether it's RAG or anything else, safety comes first.

This isn't 'innovation.' This is a 'scam.' Doctors need to wake up and fight. Patients need to be vigilant and monitor. Otherwise, we will all be caught up in a massive fraud called AI healthcare.
